# Compensation Platform Research

## Objective

This research compares leading compensation transparency platforms and identifies the core workflows and architecture decisions implemented in LevelLens.

---

# Platforms Researched

- Levels.fyi
- 6figr
- AmbitionBox
- Glassdoor

---

# Key Observations

## Levels.fyi
- Strong structured compensation system
- Excellent comparison workflows
- Level-based compensation hierarchy
- High-quality analytics dashboards
- Engineering-focused UX
- Clean normalization of companies and levels

### Strengths
- Compensation comparison
- Structured levels
- Accurate segmentation
- Data-driven workflows

### Weaknesses
- Complex UX for new users
- Limited anonymous contribution flexibility

---

## 6figr
- Focused primarily on salary discovery
- Simpler UX
- Less structured engineering level mapping
- Heavy focus on compensation bragging/social proof

### Strengths
- Easy salary discovery
- Fast contribution workflow

### Weaknesses
- Weak comparison workflows
- Limited analytics depth
- Minimal structured level system

---

## AmbitionBox
- Broad company review ecosystem
- Salary insights mixed with reviews/interviews
- Strong India-focused dataset

### Strengths
- Large company database
- Strong discoverability
- Community-generated content

### Weaknesses
- Compensation visualization less structured
- Limited comparison capabilities
- No engineering-centric leveling system

---

## Glassdoor
- Massive review platform
- Broad salary and interview data
- Generalized experience platform

### Strengths
- Large-scale data coverage
- Strong employer branding data

### Weaknesses
- Compensation workflows not engineering-focused
- Weak structured compensation comparisons
- UI can feel cluttered

# Product Decisions for LevelLens

Based on research, LevelLens prioritizes:

- Structured compensation intelligence
- Clean comparison workflows
- Engineering-focused UX
- Normalized company-level architecture
- Scalable backend APIs
- Analytics-driven dashboards

---

# Scope Decisions

LevelLens intentionally prioritizes:
- architecture quality
- reliability
- extensibility
- structured compensation systems

instead of:
- excessive animations
- social features
- overengineered UI complexity

---

# Final Takeaways

The research showed that the strongest compensation platforms share:
- normalized compensation data
- structured level systems
- comparison workflows
- analytics dashboards
- scalable backend architectures

LevelLens was designed to implement these principles in a clean and extensible MVP architecture.
